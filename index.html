Who We Are
About MVSO
A community united by music since 1989

The Miami Valley Symphony Orchestra is a community-based, all-volunteer 501(c)(3) nonprofit based in Dayton, Ohio. For over three decades, we have brought together accomplished musicians to provide high-quality performances that entertain, enrich, and inspire.

What began as a small ensemble of passionate musicians in 1989 has grown into one of the region's most respected community orchestras, performing works spanning centuries — from orchestral masterworks and film scores to Broadway favorites and contemporary compositions.

Every musician in our orchestra is a volunteer — professionals, educators, students, and lifelong music lovers who donate their time because they believe in the transformative power of live orchestral music.

Conductors
The hands that shape our sound
Principal Conductor

Awadagin Pratt

Awadagin Pratt is a musician of immense versatility and vision. A graduate of the Peabody Conservatory — the first student to receive diplomas in three performance areas: piano, violin, and conducting — he has been recognized with the Avery Fisher Career Grant and numerous other honors.

As MVSO's Principal Conductor, Pratt brings a dynamic, deeply expressive approach to the podium. His programming is bold, thoughtful, and reflective of his commitment to making orchestral music relevant and alive for today's audiences.

Our History
Three decades of orchestral excellence
1989
Pat Carson and Keith Prentice founded the MVSO. The inaugural concert, conducted by Ron Kindell, took place December 9 at Clark Haines Theatre in Kettering — a bold declaration that Dayton deserved a community orchestra of its own.
The 1990s
The orchestra grew rapidly, attracting musicians from across the region. Regular concert seasons established MVSO as a cornerstone of Dayton's cultural life.
The 2000s
Under Charles Wendelken-Wilson, MVSO expanded its artistic ambitions — blending beloved classics with lesser-known masterworks and building a reputation for adventurous programming.
2015
David Deitrick was appointed Music Director, ushering in a new era with themed concerts, guest artist collaborations, and expanded outreach.
2023
After eight years of inspired leadership, David Deitrick concluded his tenure. The orchestra began an exciting search for its next artistic leader.
Today
With Awadagin Pratt as Principal Conductor, MVSO enters a bold new chapter. More than 70 musicians continue the tradition at venues across the region.

Auditions
Your seat in the orchestra awaits

MVSO is always seeking talented, committed musicians who share our passion for orchestral excellence. Whether you're a seasoned professional or an advanced community player, we'd love to hear from you.

What to Prepare

  • One-to-two minute unaccompanied excerpt
  • Specific excerpts may be required by instrument
  • Sight-reading may be included
  • All standard orchestral instruments welcome

Rehearsal Schedule

  • Thursday evenings, 7:00–9:30 PM
  • Additional rehearsals during performance weeks
  • Concerts throughout the season
  • Commitment to full concert cycles expected

Ready to audition?

Contact our personnel manager for instrument-specific details and scheduling.

Building the Future

The Future Sounds Brilliant

MVSO is committed to developing the next generation of orchestral musicians. Our youth programming provides young musicians the opportunity to experience the thrill of a full symphony orchestra under professional guidance.

Through mentorship, side-by-side performances, and dedicated youth concerts, we create pathways for young musicians to grow their skills, build confidence, and discover the joy of ensemble playing.
